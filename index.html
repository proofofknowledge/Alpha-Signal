Chart Guide

Choosing the right timeframe

The timeframe you use dramatically affects the patterns you'll see and how you should interpret them.

Long Term — 1+ Years

Weekly & Monthly Charts

  • ✦ Use weekly or monthly candles
  • ✦ Show 2–5 years of price history
  • ✦ Patterns are stronger and more reliable
  • ✦ Filters out short-term noise
  • ✦ Best for position trading and investing
  • ✦ Key indicators: 50/200 week MA, volume

A head and shoulders pattern on a weekly chart carries far more weight than the same pattern on a 15-minute chart.

Short Term — Under 1 Year

Daily & Hourly Charts

  • ✦ Use daily candles as your primary view
  • ✦ Show 3–12 months of price history
  • ✦ 1H and 4H charts for entry timing
  • ✦ Higher frequency = more patterns, more noise
  • ✦ Best for swing trading (days to weeks)
  • ✦ Key indicators: 20/50 day MA, RSI, volume

Daily charts are the sweet spot for most retail traders — enough signal to act on without the chaos of intraday noise.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about Alpha Signal.

What is Alpha Signal and how does it work?▾
Alpha Signal uses AI to analyze chart screenshots you upload. Our engine identifies technical patterns — like head and shoulders, bull flags, double tops, and wedges — then explains what the pattern historically means and provides probability-based insights to help inform your trading decisions.
What kind of charts work best?▾
Candlestick charts with volume displayed give the best results. Make sure your chart is clean — minimal indicators, no excessive drawing tools, and enough price history visible (at least 3–6 months). Charts from any platform work: TradingView, thinkorswim, Webull, Robinhood, TD Ameritrade, and more.
How do I upload a chart?▾
Three ways: drag and drop an image file onto the upload area, click Browse Files to select one from your computer, or take a screenshot using your snipping tool (Win + Shift + S on Windows, Cmd + Shift + 4 on Mac) and then press Ctrl + V to paste it directly onto the page — no saving required.
Which timeframe should I use for long term investing?▾
For long term views (1+ years), use weekly or monthly candlestick charts with 2–5 years of price history visible. Patterns on higher timeframes carry significantly more weight and filter out short-term noise. Include the 50 and 200 week moving averages if possible, along with volume.
Which timeframe should I use for short term trading?▾
For short term views (under 1 year), the daily chart is your best starting point. Show 3–12 months of history. For timing specific entries, you can also upload 1-hour or 4-hour charts. Include the 20 and 50 day moving averages, RSI, and volume for the richest analysis.
What do the probability predictions mean?▾
Probability predictions are based on historical performance of identified patterns across thousands of prior occurrences. These are statistical likelihoods based on past data — not guarantees of future performance. Always use them as one input among many in your decision making process.
